Bentota Turtle Hatchery







With a legacy spanning three generations, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery stands as a testament to the unwavering dedication of the Samaraweera family. Established in the 1950s, this hatchery has become a pioneering force in preserving the delicate balance between tourism and turtle conservation in the region. Their tireless efforts in nurturing hatchlings and protecting nesting sites have garnered international recognition.Bentota is a tourist attraction, with a local airport (Bentota River Airport) and a handful of world-class hotels. It is a destination for watersports. Bentota also delivers an ancient art of healing called Ayurveda. Bentota is famous for its toddy production, an alcoholic beverage made out of coconut nectar. It also has a turtle hatchery, located on Induruwa beach.






About Us

Our foremost objective is to rescue and protect endangered turtle species from threats such as habitat loss, pollution, and poaching. By providing a safe haven for turtles to nest and ensuring the survival of hatchlings, we contribute to the long-term viability of these magnificent creatures.
